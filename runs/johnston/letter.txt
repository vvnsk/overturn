On behalf of Priya Natarajan, MD, and Cedar Grove Family Medicine, we respectfully request that Meridian Health Plan overturn the above denial and approve the requested lumbar spine MRI (CPT 72148). The denial was entered under reason code AD-01, "Requested Clinical Documentation Not Received," citing Section 4 of policy MP-RAD-014. As shown below, the clinical documentation the notice describes as absent is in fact present in the member's chart and is supplied and cited herewith, and the member independently satisfies the plan's own affirmative medical-necessity criterion. We ask that the request be approved on both grounds.

GROUND ONE — THE ADMINISTRATIVE BASIS FOR THE DENIAL IS CURED; THE DOCUMENTATION SAID TO BE MISSING IS IN THE RECORD.

The denial is administrative in nature: it rests not on a substantive review finding the criteria unmet, but on documentation purportedly "not received." Section 4 of the cited policy requires the record to document duration of symptoms; the specific conservative therapy modalities attempted and their duration; objective physical examination findings; and the clinical question the imaging is expected to answer.[1]

Two of the specifically enumerated items are documented in the attached chart and are quoted here to close the administrative gap:

- Duration of symptoms. The chart records chronic low back pain as an active problem with an onset date of 2019-10-30, establishing a symptom duration of more than six years as of the date of service.

- Conservative therapy modalities and their duration. The chart documents a supervised course of movement therapy comprising nine sessions on 2019-11-05, 2019-11-08, 2019-11-25, 2019-11-28, 2019-12-01, 2019-12-04, 2019-12-07, 2019-12-10, and 2019-12-13 — a span of approximately five and a half weeks.

Because AD-01 is a "documentation not received" determination rather than a merits denial, the deficiency is fully curable by supplying the record, which we do here. We request that the administrative denial be withdrawn and the request adjudicated on the documentation now before you. To the extent the reviewer requires additional detail on the remaining Section 4 items (objective examination findings and the clinical question), Dr. Natarajan is available to supplement the record and to address these directly at peer-to-peer review.

GROUND TWO — THE MEMBER INDEPENDENTLY SATISFIES SECTION 2.1, A MEDICALLY NECESSARY CRITERION THE DENIAL NEVER EVALUATED.

Beyond curing the administrative defect, the record affirmatively meets the plan's own standard for coverage. The policy provides that lumbar spine MRI is considered medically necessary when ANY ONE of the following criteria (2.1 through 2.4) is met... 2.1 Low back pain persisting six (6) weeks or longer that has failed a documented trial of conservative therapy, where conservative therapy includes at least one of: physical therapy, NSAID or analgesic pharmacotherapy, or a supervised home exercise program.[2]

The record satisfies each element of 2.1:

1. Low back pain persisting six weeks or longer. The member's chronic low back pain carries an onset date of 2019-10-30 and remains on the active problem list, far exceeding the six-week threshold.

2. A documented trial of conservative therapy. The chart documents a supervised movement therapy course of nine sessions between 2019-11-05 and 2019-12-13, a form of conservative, supervised therapy of the kind Section 2.1 contemplates.

3. Failure of that trial. The member's low back pain remains a documented active, chronic problem years after that therapy course concluded, establishing that conservative management did not resolve the condition.

Section 2.1 requires only that a qualifying criterion "be met and documented"; it does not require objective neurologic findings, which are elements of the separate criterion 2.3. The member accordingly qualifies under 2.1 on the face of the policy text and the chart's own entries.

TIMELINESS

This appeal is timely. The policy permits a written appeal, and the denial notice sets an appeal deadline of 2027-01-10. This submission is filed well within that period.

CONCLUSION

For the reasons above, the documentation cited as missing is present, and the member independently meets Section 2.1's medically-necessary standard. We respectfully request that Meridian Health Plan overturn the denial and approve MRI of the lumbar spine (CPT 72148).
