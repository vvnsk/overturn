Dear Appeals Reviewer:

On behalf of Priya Natarajan, MD, and our patient Ms. Cherly Jacobi, Cedar Grove Family Medicine respectfully requests that Meridian Health Plan overturn the above denial and approve the requested lumbar spine MRI (CPT 72148). The denial states that the record "does not establish that the member has completed a documented six (6) week trial of conservative therapy" under Medical Policy MP-RAD-014, Section 2.1. As demonstrated below, the medical record in fact documents that trial in full, and the member clearly satisfies the cited criterion.

Ground 1 — The Denial Is Factually Incorrect on Its Own Cited Criterion (Section 2.1)

The policy provides that lumbar spine MRI is medically necessary when low back pain persisting six (6) weeks or longer has failed a documented trial of conservative therapy, where conservative therapy includes at least one of: physical therapy, NSAID or analgesic pharmacotherapy, or a supervised home exercise program.[1] The record satisfies every element of this criterion.

First, as to duration of symptoms: Ms. Jacobi carries a longstanding diagnosis of chronic low back pain with an onset date of 2014-02-02, far exceeding the six-week threshold.

Second, as to a documented, failed trial of conservative therapy: the faxed Physical Therapy Discharge Summary from Riverbend Rehabilitation documents supervised physical therapy for chronic low back pain, 14 visits over 7 weeks (2026-05-12 through 2026-07-01).[2] This course exceeds the six-week requirement by a full week. Adherence was excellent, documented at 13/14 visits.[3]

Notably, the member satisfies not one but all three of the qualifying conservative-therapy modalities named in Section 2.1, where the policy requires only one. The physical therapy course itself encompassed lumbar stabilization, graded activity, and a supervised home exercise program,[4] and concurrent NSAID pharmacotherapy was in place, as naproxen 500mg BID was maintained throughout the course per PCP.[5]

Third, as to failure of that trial: the discharge note records that the patient completed the course with minimal symptomatic improvement, with low back pain persisting with functional limitation.[6] The treating therapist accordingly referred the patient back to the ordering physician for advanced imaging evaluation.[7]

The single fact the denial identifies as missing — a documented six-week conservative therapy trial — is present in the record, is longer than six weeks, is supervised, and has failed. The denial is factually erroneous on the very criterion it cites, and Section 2.1 is met.

Ground 2 — The Documentation Requirements of Section 4 Are Satisfied

The policy requires that the record document duration of symptoms; the specific conservative therapy modalities attempted and their duration; objective physical examination findings; and the clinical question the imaging is expected to answer.[8] The attached chart supplies the symptom duration (chronic low back pain since 2014), the specific modalities and their duration (14 PT visits over 7 weeks with lumbar stabilization, graded activity, and home exercise, plus maintained naproxen therapy), and the clinical question (evaluation of persistent low back pain that has failed conservative management, per the therapist's referral for advanced imaging). The documentation standard of Section 4 is met.

Timeliness

This appeal is submitted on July 18, 2026, well within the appeal window; the denial notice states an appeal deadline of January 4, 2027.

Conclusion

Because the member independently satisfies Section 2.1 — the sole basis of the denial — with a documented, supervised, failed conservative therapy trial exceeding six weeks in a patient with chronic low back pain since 2014, we respectfully request that Meridian Health Plan overturn the denial and approve CPT 72148. We welcome the opportunity for a peer-to-peer discussion between the ordering physician, Dr. Priya Natarajan, and a plan medical director, which the denial notice indicates is available.
